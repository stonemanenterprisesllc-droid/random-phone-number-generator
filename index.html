How the numbers are generated

US phone numbers follow the North American Numbering Plan (NANP): (NPA) NXX-XXXX — a 3-digit area code, a 3-digit exchange, and a 4-digit line number. The first digit of both the area code and the exchange must be 2–9 (never 0 or 1). This generator respects those rules, so every number looks valid to any format checker — but the full 7-digit combination is random, and we make no attempt to match numbers actually assigned to people. Everything runs in your browser; nothing is stored or sent anywhere.

Picking a format

Use (555) 123-4567 for US-facing UI mockups, 5551234567 (plain) for database seeding and CSV imports, and +1 555 123 4567 (E.164-style) when testing international dialing or SMS APIs. The Copy All button copies one number per line, ready to paste into a spreadsheet.

Responsible use

Generated numbers are fictitious samples. Don't dial or text them, don't publish them as contact information, and don't use them to create accounts or bypass phone verification — that violates most services' terms and our Terms of Use. For guaranteed-fictional numbers in film or documentation, the classic 555-01XX exchange is also a safe choice.
